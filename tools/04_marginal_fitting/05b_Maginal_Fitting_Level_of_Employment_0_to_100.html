Fixing Methodology¶

  • The distribution of level of employement of active people in 2017 will be reproduced in 2040
  • In 2040, too many people are working 100%
    • Some of the people working 100% will be sampled out randomly
    • Their level of employment will be set to a lower value

Computing how many people to modify¶

In [31]:
tot_employed_ist = (persons_ist['level_of_employment'] > 0).sum()
tot_full_time_ist = (persons_ist['level_of_employment'] == 100).sum()
full_time_proportion_ist = tot_full_time_ist / tot_employed_ist

print('In {year_ist}, {pc_ft:.0f} % of working people work full-time.'
      .format(year_ist=YEAR_IST, pc_ft=full_time_proportion_ist * 100))

tot_employed = (persons['level_of_employment'] > 0).sum()
tot_full_time = (persons['level_of_employment'] == 100).sum()
full_time_proportion = tot_full_time / tot_employed

print('In {year_ist} (raw), {pc_ft:.0f} % of working people work full-time.'
      .format(year_ist=YEAR, pc_ft=full_time_proportion * 100))

expected_tot_full_time =int(tot_employed * full_time_proportion_ist)
nbr_persons_to_modify = tot_full_time - expected_tot_full_time

print('\nIn order to match the proportions from {year_ist}, {nbr_p} full-time workers must be modified!'
      .format(year_ist=YEAR_IST, nbr_p=nbr_persons_to_modify))

# Sampling a new level of employement from the loe distributions of all part-time workers
new_loe = reference_distribution.sample(nbr_persons_to_modify, replace=True, weights=reference_distribution).index.values

# From the people working full-time, sample out the ones to change
person_pool = persons_fixed.query('level_of_employment == 100')['person_id'].values


# Selection the persons to be changes out of the pool of candidates
